Advice on Selecting a Wok to Buy


What type of wok should you buy?

There is no substitute for a good carbon steel wok. Not only is it relatively inexpensive, but it conducts heat evenly. If you are cooking with a electric hob, your best option is to use a flat bottom wok. Round bottom woks can reflect heat back on the element, damaging it. A flat-bottomed wok can also be used on gas stoves.

Handle, one or two?

Originally, woks came with two metal handles. Today, flat bottom woks normally have a long wooden handle, like a skillet. The long handle makes it easy to move and tilt the wok when stir-frying. One or two, it's up to you really.

Non-stick or not?

With the current interest in low-fat cooking, many manufacturers now offer woks with a non-stick coating. How well non-stick works will depend on the individual coating and how it is applied. Choose a Wok With a Lid

A wok with a lid will make your life a lot easier especially when you cook Chinese food. A lot of time the ingredients need to simmer and be covered during cooking.

Taking care of your wok


Wash the wok in hot water with a small amount of liquid detergent and a scrubber. Do not use the abrasive cleanser on the inside of the wok.
Rinse the wok, dry thoroughly and then place the wok on high heat.
Move the wok, turning it and tilting it until the metal turns a blueish-yellowish color.
Remove the wok from the heat and turn the heat down to medium-low.
Add a thin film of oil over the entire inside surface of the wok with a paper towel.
Heat the wok on medium-low heat for about 10 minutes.
Wipe off the oil with another paper towel. There will be black residue on the towel.
Repeat the oil, heat and wipe steps until no black residue comes off on the paper (about 3 times).
The wok is now ready to use.
